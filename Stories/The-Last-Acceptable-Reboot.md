# The Last Acceptable Reboot

When every prophecy printer names junior auditor [[Tess Quill]] as the temporary chosen one, she must inspect the failing [[Continuity Engine]] before [[The Executive Producer]] compresses reality into a cleaner, younger reboot.

Tess chooses whether to cooperate with the [[Ministry of Narrative Compliance]], the [[Freeplot Cooperative]], or [[Continuity Studios]]. With help from [[Captain Sol Ledger]], [[Professor Beige]], [[Sprig-Nine]], and [[Paladin Patch]], she gathers four genre credentials and reaches the final console beneath [[Finale Galleria]].

## Themes

- Shared canon versus personal agency.
- Affectionate parody of genre infrastructure.
- The difference between maintaining continuity and owning it.
- Whether a satisfying ending must be definitive.

## Endings

The modular PathBranching workspace provides six outcomes: Patched Canon, Stories in Common, Prestige Reboot, Executive Promotion, Eternal Sequel, and the secret Ordinary Lunch ending.
